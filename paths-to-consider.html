A Few Paths That Might Be Worth Looking At

I pulled these together because the usual conversation can get too narrow too fast.

This is not meant as "here is the plan." It is just a way to react to a few concrete options that seem more interesting than a generic university-versus-nothing discussion.

You do not need to like all of them. The useful part is noticing what attracts you, what repels you, and what feels dead on arrival.

If what attracts you is fintech or finance

You could look at paths like:

  • Wise Early Careers
  • Adyen Early Careers
  • Revolut Graduate Programme
  • Investment20/20 Trainee Programme

Why they may be worth a look:

  • real companies
  • international environment
  • serious people
  • not only coding roles
  • a way to see whether fintech or finance is actually interesting in real life

If what attracts you is building things with strong peers

You could look at:

  • 42 Berlin
  • Recurse Center

Why they may be worth a look:

  • strong technical environment
  • people who care about making things
  • nontraditional path
  • a clearer test than just thinking about ideas alone

If what attracts you is learning from a strong adult while building something real

You could look at:

  • Erasmus for Young Entrepreneurs
  • Santander X Explorer

Why they may be worth a look:

  • external mentorship
  • real project or business context
  • more grounded than just reading or talking

If what attracts you is challenge, crew, and a different environment

You could look at:

  • Tall Ships Youth Trust Start Yachting
  • Ocean Youth Trust South
  • UKSA

Why they may be worth a look:

  • real-world challenge
  • responsibility
  • a stronger social environment than ordinary drift
  • confidence built by doing things, not just talking about them

If what attracts you is a short reset before deciding the next step

You could look at:

  • Outward Bound Pathfinder
  • Raleigh International Expeditions

Why they may be worth a look:

  • clear beginning, middle, and end
  • challenge without pretending the whole future must be decided now
  • a way to step out of the current atmosphere and then reassess

What Would Actually Be Useful

It would probably be more useful to react to a few real options than to debate everything in the abstract.

For example:

  • which of these feels genuinely interesting
  • which of these feels impressive but not actually alive
  • which of these puts you around the kind of people you would want more of

A Simple Way To Do This

If you want, pick just three:

  • one finance or fintech path
  • one builder path
  • one challenge or sailing path

Then compare only those three.

That is usually more useful than trying to solve everything in one shot.
