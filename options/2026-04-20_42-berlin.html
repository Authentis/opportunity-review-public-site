Why It May Be Interesting

  • It addresses several core needs at once: stronger peers, structure, proof through work, and an English-speaking environment relatively close to home.
  • It does not require a traditional degree path or prior coding credentials.
  • Berlin is culturally more international than many Dutch options and may feel less deadening to you.
  • The admissions process tests commitment and problem-solving more than credentials.

Things To Check

  • The 42 model is still demanding and can become overwhelming if self-direction collapses.
  • It is a substantial commitment, not a short trial.
  • It is software-engineering focused, not specifically finance or entrepreneurship focused.
  • Passing the selection process requires real stamina and willingness to be a beginner in public.
