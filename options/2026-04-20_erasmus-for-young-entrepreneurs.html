At A Glance

What it is1 to 6 month exchange with an experienced host entrepreneur
OrganisationEuropean Commission / Erasmus for Young Entrepreneurs
LocationCross-border within participating countries, with global extensions in some cases
LanguageVaries by host; generally workable in English for many placements
TimingOngoing programme; no fixed registration deadline; exchange length is 1 to 6 months
Costlow; EU financial support helps cover travel and living costs
EligibilityFor new entrepreneurs who are firmly planning to start a business or have run one for less than 3 years; requires a serious business plan, CV, motivation, and residence in a participating country.
Application deadlineNo fixed programme deadline; matching and local-contact-point approval happen on a rolling basis.
Application routeRegister on the Erasmus for Young Entrepreneurs platform, choose a local contact point, submit CV / motivation / business plan, then search and match with a host entrepreneur.
May appeal ifYou may like this if you want to learn from a respected external adult while testing entrepreneurship in a real business setting

Why It May Be Interesting

  • It gives you a credible adult model outside your usual environment.
  • It is structured, practical, and based on working with a real host entrepreneur.
  • It could reduce direct comparison pressure by moving learning into a different relationship.
  • It is more human and reality-based than a purely online founder program.

Things To Check

  • You need a concrete business idea or viable business plan.
  • The quality of the experience depends heavily on the host match.
  • It is entrepreneurship-oriented, not specifically fintech.
  • It assumes you can engage seriously once placed, not just admire the idea.
