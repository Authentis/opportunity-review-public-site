Why It May Be Interesting

  • It is English-speaking and finance-adjacent.
  • It has work, study, professional qualifications, and employer structure.
  • It gives adult workplace rhythm rather than abstract university study alone.
  • It is more accessible in principle than elite finance or UK-only routes if Ireland work rights are straightforward.
  • It may provide mentoring and a supervisor rather than relying only on self-direction.

Things To Check

  • It is insurance / financial advice rather than fintech, trading, or startup finance.
  • It is a three-year commitment, so it is not a light test.
  • You would need to respect ordinary professional training and client-service work.
  • Open employers, exact entry-equivalence, and nationality / right-to-work must be checked.
  • It may not feel prestigious enough unless framed as a paid route into financial competence.
