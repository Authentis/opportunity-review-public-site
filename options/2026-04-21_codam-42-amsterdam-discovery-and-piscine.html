Why It May Be Interesting

  • It gives a short, concrete test before a large Berlin decision.
  • It is free and close enough to try without relocation.
  • It offers peer-to-peer learning and visible challenge.
  • The full Codam / 42 route still has recognition through the global 42 Network.
  • The 5-day Discovery Piscine could answer whether coding is genuinely alive as an interest.

Things To Check

  • It is still in the Netherlands, which may reduce appeal.
  • It is coding-focused, not finance-focused.
  • The full Codam curriculum is full-time, around 40 hours per week, and can take about two years.
  • Peer-to-peer learning can be challenging if you need more direct teaching or guidance.
  • Codam cannot provide student visas, though this is probably not relevant if you are an EU / Schengen citizen.
