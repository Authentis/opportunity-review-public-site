Why It May Be Interesting

  • It sits in the middle ground between early startup interest and full founder intensity.
  • It gives you a structured way to see what founder discipline feels like in practice.
  • Weekly pitch feedback and practical sprints could turn startup interest into visible proof.
  • Berlin is more international and peer-rich than many local default options.

Things To Check

  • The official page says the program is hard by design and that only a minority finish.
  • It asks for roughly 25 hours per week, which is a real commitment.
  • Repeated feedback and evaluation could trigger the very beginner-status intolerance already noted in the project.
  • It still assumes you are serious enough to stay in the work once the excitement wears off.
