Why It May Be Interesting

  • It keeps academia alive without forcing you back into a conventional campus model.
  • It offers the kind of global, high-agency peer environment you seems to imagine.
  • It combines rigorous academics with projects, internships, and real-world exposure.
  • It could fit your mix of math, AI, business, and broad curiosity better than a narrow standard program.

Things To Check

  • It is not a low-pressure environment; it looks demanding and socially intense.
  • Frequent relocation and adaptation could be energizing or destabilizing depending on your actual resilience.
  • It is still a degree path, which may trigger resistance if you hears only "university" and not the actual structure.
  • Cost and funding need proper verification before treating it as realistic.
