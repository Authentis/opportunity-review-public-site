Possible Next Steps

Audience: Shareable with Maxim.<br> Purpose: A respectful shortlist of possible next-step environments and paths to react to, not a diagnosis or a decision made for him.<br> Last updated: 20 April 2026

This note is simple on purpose.

It is not saying what you "should" do. It is just a map of a few options that seem more alive and realistic than a generic university-or-nothing discussion.

The real question is not only "what job?"

It is also:

  • what kind of environment would you actually want to be in
  • what kind of people would you respect
  • what kind of challenge would feel worth taking seriously

A Few Real Options

1. Fintech Employer Path

Examples:

  • Wise Early Careers
  • Adyen Early Careers
  • Revolut Graduate Programme

Why this may be worth looking at:

  • real companies
  • international environment
  • stronger peers than a random local setting
  • not only coding roles
  • possible entry into payments, finance, operations, product, or business-side work

Good if you want:

  • a serious environment
  • smart people
  • a path that is practical, not abstract

2. Finance Entry With Training

Example:

  • Investment20/20 Trainee Programme

Why this may be worth looking at:

  • more structured than applying cold to elite finance jobs
  • actual development and training
  • a way into finance without pretending to already be finished

Good if you want:

  • finance exposure
  • real standards
  • a coached start rather than pure sink-or-swim

3. External Mentor + Build Something

Examples:

  • Erasmus for Young Entrepreneurs
  • Santander X Explorer

Why this may be worth looking at:

  • external mentorship
  • real projects and real business context
  • less passive than just reading or thinking about ideas

Good if you want:

  • independence with structure
  • serious adult guidance
  • a path closer to building than to sitting in class

4. Intense Builder Community

Examples:

  • 42 Berlin
  • Recurse Center

Why this may be worth looking at:

  • strong peers
  • nontraditional path
  • clear test of whether an intense builder environment actually fits

Good if you want:

  • a serious technical environment
  • people who care about making things
  • a clean break from ordinary university atmosphere

5. Structured Sailing Challenge

Examples:

  • Tall Ships Youth Trust Start Yachting
  • Ocean Youth Trust South
  • UKSA

Why this may be worth looking at:

  • challenge with a real crew and real responsibility
  • stronger environment than a vague "reset"
  • confidence built through doing, not talking

Good if you want:

  • movement
  • real-world challenge
  • a different kind of peer group

6. Short Reset With Structure

Examples:

  • Outward Bound Pathfinder
  • Raleigh International Expeditions

Why this may be worth looking at:

  • not endless drift
  • clear beginning, middle, and end
  • useful if the next step is not yet clear but staying stuck also feels wrong

Good if you want:

  • space to reset
  • challenge
  • a more solid next decision afterward

What Seems Most Worth Testing First

If the goal is not to waste time, the most promising first questions may be:

  1. Does a serious fintech or finance environment actually feel interesting in reality, not just in theory?
  2. Does a strong in-person peer group make you more alive and focused?
  3. Is sailing a real attraction or more of a symbol of freedom and reset?
  4. Do you want to build things, join a strong team, or first reset your environment?

Three Useful Questions

  • Which of these options feels genuinely interesting, not just impressive?
  • Which one would you still respect even if it became hard after two weeks?
  • Which one puts you around the kind of people you actually want more of?

Practical Next Step

If useful, pick just three things:

  • one fintech or finance path
  • one builder or mentorship path
  • one sailing or reset path

Then compare only those three in detail.

That is usually better than trying to decide from twenty options at once.
