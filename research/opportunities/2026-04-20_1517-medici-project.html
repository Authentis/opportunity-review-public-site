Summary

1517 is unusually aligned with young people outside traditional credential paths. It supports dropouts, students, hackers, makers, and early founders. The Medici Project is a lightweight route into their world through a project-centered grant and support process.

Why It May Fit

  • The philosophy is unusually compatible with someone who distrusts standard educational status signals.
  • It values ambition, curiosity, and project-building over credentials.
  • It offers both belief capital and access to peers, mentors, and future support.
  • It could be psychologically powerful if the participant responds well to being taken seriously on a real project.

Risks / Constraints

  • 1517 says direct grants are limited to teams based in North America because of regulatory constraints.
  • They still welcome people outside North America to write in, but support may be limited.
  • This is not a structured daily environment by itself.
  • It works best if the participant already has a concrete project the participant can explain credibly.

Recommended Next Step

  • Keep this as a useful external network and mentorship lead, not a primary structure solution.
  • Only pursue it if the participant has a real project worth describing in a short Loom video or written pitch.
  • Treat it as a potential belief-and-network option, especially if the participant's project starts to become real.
