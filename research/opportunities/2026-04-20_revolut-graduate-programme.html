Summary

Revolut is a real early-career fintech path and broader than pure engineering. The official pages show internships in areas like Operations, Product, Design, Data Science, and Security, and a 12-month graduate program for recent and upcoming graduates. This is more plausible than Claude's memo implied for a non-engineering route, but eligibility still matters a lot.

Why It May Fit

  • It is directly in fintech and clearly international.
  • The official pages present more than one path beyond coding.
  • The graduate program is friendlier to recent graduates than a penultimate-year-only internship model.
  • It offers a live test of whether the participant likes real operational intensity or only the idea of fintech.

Risks / Constraints

  • The internship explicitly requires penultimate-year university status.
  • The graduate program is aimed at recent and upcoming graduates, so the participant's exact status still matters.
  • Revolut's culture may be exciting for some people and too high-pressure for others.

Recommended Next Step

  • Keep this as a serious fintech watch-option.
  • Check whether a young adult would qualify for the graduate program rather than the internship.
  • If the participant's interest in fintech stays strong, monitor non-coding functions rather than defaulting to software roles.
