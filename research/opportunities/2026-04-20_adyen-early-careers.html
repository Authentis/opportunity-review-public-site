Summary

Adyen is one of the strongest fintech employers in the Netherlands and remains relevant even if a young adult does not want a traditional Dutch-only environment. The official early-careers page shows three routes: NextGen for final-year students, internship projects for master’s students, and graduate roles for recent graduates with up to two years of experience.

Why It May Fit

  • It is directly in fintech and payments rather than generic tech.
  • It has serious exposure to money flows, risk, operations, and financial products.
  • It sits in Amsterdam, which is practical geographically even if local language fit is imperfect.
  • Graduate roles are broader than only coding and can lead into finance-adjacent work.

Risks / Constraints

  • The page currently showed no open job listings when checked.
  • Adyen’s NextGen and internship paths are still student-linked.
  • Company standards appear high and self-direction is expected.
  • It is a strong option only if the participant is willing to operate in a demanding professional environment.

Recommended Next Step

  • Treat Adyen as a strong fintech employer to monitor, not as an immediate guaranteed route.
  • Check whether the graduate-role path is realistic based on the participant's university status.
  • If fintech is a true priority, add Adyen to a watched set of companies rather than searching randomly.
