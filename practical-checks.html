1. Sailing: RYA Certificate First

Your first sailing idea was clear: RYA Competent Crew, or possibly a higher / more specific module if a provider says your level fits.

So the first check should be the formal RYA route. Tall Ships still matters, but more as one possible sailing environment than as the whole sailing path.

Is Competent Crew the right first certificate, or should you ask a provider whether a higher / more specific module makes sense?

2. Ecole 42: Broader Campus Discovery

The 42 path is not only Berlin. It is a network, and the right first check may be about city, timing, language, energy, and how heavy the commitment feels.

Your logic seems to be:

  1. test whether the 42 style fits, possibly in the Netherlands first;
  2. compare the campuses that actually interest you;
  3. if it still looks right, dedicate a month to a Piscine;
  4. then consider the full path, including the qualification route and the large-city / international network.

The useful thing is to compare a few campuses, not all of them forever.

3. Cold Outreach: 1517 And Similar People

This is the easiest action to do without travel or commitment.

1517 Medici Project is relevant because it takes young builders seriously. The catch is that direct grant support may be limited outside North America, so the first ask should not be money.

Better first ask:

  • feedback on what proof would matter;
  • a 15-minute conversation;
  • a pointer to someone more relevant;
  • advice on what a serious first project should look like.

Draft checklist:

CheckGood version
LengthShort enough to read in under one minute.
ToneCurious, specific, direct.
AskOne small ask, not an open-ended request for help.
ProofOne concrete interest, project idea, or thing you are testing.
Follow-upOne polite follow-up after a week or so.

First action:

Draft one email and send it to 2 or 3 people.

4. Ireland IFS: Real But Employer-Dependent

Ireland IFS is a finance apprenticeship route, not a normal university application. It usually means paid work plus study through National College of Ireland and an employer.

Useful links:

  • Ireland International Financial Services Apprenticeship
  • LIA / Insurance Practitioner Apprenticeship Ireland
  • Investment20/20 Trainee Programme

What to check:

CheckWhy it matters
Employer openingThere is not one central application that solves everything.
Work rightIreland may be easier than the UK, but it still needs checking.
Grade equivalenceEmployers ask for Irish Leaving Certificate equivalents.
CV readinessSome listings ask for grades directly in the CV.
TimingSome routes fill on a rolling basis.

First action:

Find one current employer opening and check whether you can apply now.
